I am a PhD researcher in the [Science for AI & AI for Science 2](https://www.birmingham.ac.uk) group at the **University of Birmingham**, supervised within the Department of Computer Science. My research sits at the intersection of **quantum computing** and **machine learning**, with a particular focus on quantum generative modelling, drug discovery, and parameter-efficient fine-tuning of large language models.

My work explores how near-term quantum devices (NISQ hardware) can be harnessed to build more powerful and sample-efficient generative models. I am especially interested in hybrid quantum–classical architectures — combining the representational power of quantum circuits with the scalability of classical deep learning — to tackle real-world challenges in molecular generation and drug discovery.

**Research Interests:**
- Quantum generative modelling (Quantum GANs, VAEs, diffusion models)
- Hybrid quantum–classical machine learning on NISQ devices
- Molecular generation and drug discovery via quantum–AI
- Parameter-efficient fine-tuning of large language models

I hold an **MSci in Theoretical Physics (1st Class Honours)** from **Imperial College London**, where my master's thesis focused on *Neural Predictor-Guided Quantum Ansatz Search*. I have had the privilege of interning at the **National Institute of Informatics (NII), Tokyo**, working on graph machine learning for molecular generation, and at **QunaSys (Copenhagen)**, contributing to the [QuEnAIS](https://qunasys.com) and FULLMAP consortiums on quantum-enhanced AI for drug and battery discovery.

Earlier in my career I worked with **DeepWok Lab at Imperial College London** on parameter-efficient fine-tuning, co-authoring a long paper accepted at **ACL 2025** on salience-aware sparse fine-tuning of LLMs.

Outside research, I run a private tuition business, am a landscape photographer, an amateur boxer, a gym enthusiast, and play the piano.
